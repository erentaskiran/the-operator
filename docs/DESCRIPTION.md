# The Operator

**You don't catch liars. You read them.**

A lamp lit room. A polygraph. A suspect with a rehearsed story and a body that might betray them. You are the Operator, and the court is waiting on your call.

## The Job

Every case is a civil suit waiting on your verdict. A pharmacist accused of diluting chemotherapy batches. A surgeon whose patient never woke up. A developer whose building came down. The lawyers have signed off. The suspect is in the chair. The rest is up to you.

## The Readout

Three signals. Pulse. Breath. Skin.

No labels. No hints. No little words telling you when they are lying. Just raw waveforms climbing and falling across the screen while they talk. A spike can mean guilt, or caffeine, or the SSRI in their bloodstream, or nothing at all. The dossier in your hand is the only thing that tells you which.

Read the baseline wrong and the whole session is poisoned.

## The Questions

You pick every line. Every question carries a tactic. Empathic. Aggressive. Trap. Evidence. Moral pressure. Legal threat. The right tactic cracks them open. The wrong tactic lets them reframe, or calls their lawyer back into the room, and the window closes for good.

Each suspect has pressure points buried in the dossier. Use their daughter's name at the wrong moment and they harden. Use it at the right moment and the pulse line jumps off the rail.

## The Verdict

At the end you call it. Guilty or not guilty. The truth comes out either way. You find out what you missed, what you imagined, and what the waveform was telling you all along.

## What It Feels Like

Quiet tension. Pixel art rooms and soft room tone. A typewriter ticking out narration. The scratch of the polygraph pen. A stress meter you cannot see inside their head, only infer from the traces under your hand.

Two languages. Branching dialogue. Cases that reward patience and punish guessing.

**Who is lying, and who is just afraid?**
